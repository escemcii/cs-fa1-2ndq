Morning Routine

My day as a sports car dealer starts early. I open the showroom, check the cars on display, and make sure each one is spotless and ready for clients. I also review my appointments for the day and go over inventory reports.

Midday Tasks & Projects

By midday, the dealership gets busier. I meet potential buyers, introduce them to different car models, and discuss features and performance details. It’s where my knowledge of engines, aerodynamics, and automotive technology—things I learned from science—becomes very useful.

Even though I plan to take a science-related course in college, I want to use that background to understand how cars are built and perform. That way, I can give real, technical insights to clients while managing my own car business someday.

A Story from My Dream Job

At first, I didn’t really have much interest in cars. They just seemed like ordinary machines to me. But when I started hearing things about Formula 1, I was amazed by how fast, precise, and well-engineered those cars were. It opened my eyes to how cool cars could actually be.

Later on, I began watching car dealership videos on TikTok, especially from the page F2A Cars. Seeing how they showcased luxury and sports cars — and how much knowledge and passion they had — made me even more interested in this world. Now, I really enjoy learning about cars, and I’ve realized that car dealers, especially those who work with sports cars, can earn a lot while doing something exciting and fun.
